My passion for design started with Legos.

While I enjoyed making the detailed sets, I always instinctively left the instructions and followed my imagination. Elaborate spaceships came from typical house sets. Over the years, my creative focus widened and began to influence much of how I saw things. As a result, I found a love for graphic-design, UI/UX, architecture, and problem solving.

I’m currently a student at Harvard University (Cambridge, MA), concentrating in Computer Science and the History of Art and Architecture.

I’ve applied my design-oriented to many of the groups I’ve been a part of. I’ve worked on a series of social-media campaigns for several clubs and organizations. I’m developing and design a website for one of Harvard’s club as well. My abilities as a coder are also comparable to my design skills. I’ve done a handful of small coding-projects, most of which are in Java. However, I do know: C, HTML, CSS, and Python.

My professional experience is limited; however, I’m looking constantly looking for opportunities to apply my creative mindset to any willing organization. My thirst for both knowledge and bettering the world is ever-present.
